## Design Context

### Users
Affluent car enthusiasts and collectors, primarily 40-65 years old, based in Germany (DACH region) with international reach (France, Switzerland). They arrive either through search ("Ferrari kaufen Freiburg"), referral, or direct. They are comparing 2-3 dealers and want to feel confident this is a serious, trustworthy operation — not a flashy reseller. They value discretion, expertise, and personal service. Many are repeat buyers or come for workshop services on existing vehicles.

### Brand Personality
**Understated. Authoritative. Personal.**

- Voice: Confident but never boastful. States facts, doesn't sell. The cars speak for themselves.
- Tone: Formal "Sie" in German, warm but professional. Like a private banker, not a car salesman.
- Emotional goals: Walking into a private showroom — quiet confidence, curated exclusivity, the feeling that every vehicle has been personally selected.
- Reference: Patek Philippe (understated luxury), Kidston SA (automotive authority), RM Sotheby's (curated presentation)
- Anti-reference: Gucci (too loud), generic car dealership templates, AI-generated dark-mode-with-gold sites

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark mode primary with warm light sections for contrast. The darkness should feel like an intimate gallery, not a nightclub.
- **Typography**: Serif display font for headlines (authority, heritage) paired with clean sans-serif for body (readability, modernity). Current Geist is too generic — needs a distinctive serif like Freight Display, Canela, or similar.
- **Color**: Gold (#C8A84E) as accent is correct for the segment, but should be used sparingly — a whisper, not a shout. Warm neutrals over cold grays. No pure black — use near-black with warm undertone.
- **Photography**: Real vehicle photography is essential (currently stock). When real assets arrive, tight detail crops signal premium. Wide environmental shots signal scale.
- **Layout**: Asymmetric compositions, generous whitespace (120px+ section padding), cinematic aspect ratios (21:9 heroes). Break the 3-column grid monotony. One dominant element per viewport.
- **Motion**: Subtle, physics-based. Smooth deceleration (ease-out-expo), no bounce. Staggered reveals on scroll. Page transitions between routes.
- **Texture**: Subtle grain overlay (3-5% opacity) to avoid flat digital sterility.

### Design Principles

1. **Show, don't decorate.** Every visual element serves the cars. If it doesn't make the vehicles look better, remove it.
2. **Earned minimalism.** Not minimal because it's easy — minimal because every remaining element is essential and polished to perfection.
3. **Authority through restraint.** No exclamation marks, no urgency tactics, no "SALE" banners. The confidence comes from what is NOT said.
4. **One thing at a time.** Each viewport moment has one clear focus. No competing elements. The scroll experience is choreographed.
5. **Real over perfect.** Authentic photography, real testimonials, genuine expertise signals over polished stock imagery and generic copy.
